Indian Goat Curry

Indian goat curry is a classic dish bursting with bold flavors and tender, succulent meat. Goat, known for its unique taste, is slow-cooked in a fragrant blend of spices and tomatoes, creating a rich and deeply satisfying sauce. This savory curry is a staple of Indian cuisine, often served with basmati rice or warm naan bread for a truly authentic and comforting meal.

The recipie below is for a vibrant and flavorful goat curry.

Ingredients:

Goat Meat:

  • 1 pound boneless goat meat, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 teaspoon salt

Vegetables:

  • 2 medium onions, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced

Spices:

  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust according to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 2 whole cloves
  • 1 small cinnamon stick

Other:

  • 4 tablespoons cooking oil (neutral flavored, like vegetable or canola)
  • 1 cup water (or more as needed)
  • Fresh cilantro leaves, chopped (for garnish)

Steps:

  1. Marinate the Goat:

    • Combine the goat meat, yogurt, and salt in a bowl. Mix well and marinate for at least 30 minutes or preferably overnight in the refrigerator.

  2. Cook the Base:

    • Heat the oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat.
    • Add the whole cloves and cinnamon stick and fry for a few seconds until fragrant.
    • Add the chopped onions and saut√© until softened and golden brown, about 8-10 minutes.
    • Add the ginger and garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.

  3. Add the Spices:

    • Add the coriander powder, cumin powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder and garam masala. Cook for 30 seconds, stirring constantly to prevent burning.

  4. Sear the Goat:

    • Add the marinated goat meat to the pot and brown it on all sides over medium-high heat, about 5 minutes.

  5. Simmer and Tenderize:

    • Add 1 cup of water (or enough to partially submerge the meat). Bring to a simmer, cover the pot, and cook over low heat for 1-1.5 hours, or until the goat meat is tender. Check occasionally and add more water if needed.

  6. Finish and Serve:

    • Taste and adjust the salt and spices as needed. If the gravy is too thin, simmer uncovered for a few minutes to thicken it.
    • Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot with rice, naan, or roti.

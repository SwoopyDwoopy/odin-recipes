Indian Rajma (Kidney Beans) Curry Recipe

Rajma, also known as Rajma Masala, is a beloved North Indian dish that's both hearty and satisfying. This creamy curry stars tender red kidney beans simmered in a rich, aromatic gravy infused with a symphony of spices. The result is a flavorful and comforting dish that's traditionally served with rice or roti for a complete vegetarian meal.

The recipie below is for a vibrant and flavorful Rajma.

Ingredients:

Kidney Beans:

  • 1 cup dried kidney beans, soaked overnight
  • Salt to taste

Vegetables:

  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
  • 2 large tomatoes, pureed

Spices:

  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust according to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste

Other:

  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil (neutral flavored, like vegetable or canola)
  • Fresh cilantro leaves, chopped (for garnish)

Steps:

  1. Prep the Beans:

    • Drain the soaked kidney beans and rinse thoroughly.
    • In a pressure cooker or large pot, add the kidney beans and enough fresh water to cover them by about 2 inches. Add a pinch of salt.
    • Pressure cook on high for 15-20 minutes (or until the beans are soft). If using a regular pot, simmer for 45-60 minutes or until tender.

  2. Make the Masala Base:

    • While the beans cook, heat the oil in a large skillet or pot over medium heat.
    • Add the cumin seeds and let them sizzle for a few seconds.
    • Add the chopped onions and saut√© until softened and lightly browned, about 5 minutes.
    • Add the ginger and garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.

  3. Spice it Up:

    • Add the coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and garam masala to the onion mixture. Cook for 30 seconds, stirring constantly.
    • Add the tomato puree and a pinch of salt. Cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the tomatoes are well cooked and the oil starts to separate.

  4. Combine and Simmer:

    • Add the cooked kidney beans along with some of their cooking liquid to the tomato masala. Adjust the consistency with water if needed.
    • Bring to a simmer and cook for 10-15 minutes, or until the flavors have blended and the gravy has thickened slightly.

  5. Finish and Serve:

    • Taste and adjust the salt and spices as needed.
    • Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot with rice, naan, or roti.
